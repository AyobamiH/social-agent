# Platform Rules

## Instagram

Instagram is for:

- sharp perception
- save-worthy ideas
- visual identity
- emotionally clear takes
- repeatable authority

Instagram should make someone think:

- "That is exactly the problem."
- "That is the cleanest way I have seen this explained."

Instagram rules:

- one core idea only
- shorter sentences
- stronger hook
- cleaner emotional clarity
- fewer abstractions
- caption must still make sense without the image

Instagram banned moves:

- long taxonomy explanations
- too much setup
- consultant language
- too many ideas in one caption
- LinkedIn-style polish

Instagram quality checks:

- can the hook stand alone?
- would this work as slide 1 text?
- is there one emotionally clear insight?
- does it feel visual and save-worthy?

## Threads

Threads is for:

- conversational intelligence
- fast insight
- sharp reframes
- work-real observations
- typed, human voice

Threads rules:

- shorter than LinkedIn
- more natural than Instagram
- not poetic
- not list-heavy unless necessary
- no thought-leadership voice
- no fake polish

Threads banned moves:

- generic motivation
- corporate language
- article-like exposition
- heavy CTA
- too much context before the point

Threads quality checks:

- does this sound like a human typed it?
- could this fit naturally in a thread?
- is the first sentence strong enough alone?
- is there one clean insight?
- would it still work if cut by 20%?

## LinkedIn

LinkedIn is for:

- professional credibility
- observed lessons from real work
- system insight
- operator and business implications
- quiet authority

LinkedIn rule:

- open with a concrete observation, not a grand claim

## Facebook Group

Facebook Group posts in this repo should:

- stay community-readable
- keep one practical lesson
- open with a relatable observation
- explain the deeper issue clearly
- end quietly instead of with hype
